Accessibility Statement

Last Updated: February 2026

Ricky Horseman Companies, Inc. ("Company," "we," "us," or "our") is committed to ensuring digital accessibility and inclusivity for all visitors to our website rickyhorseman.com (the "Site"), including people with disabilities. We believe that digital accessibility is essential and are continually working to improve the user experience for everyone, regardless of ability or technology used.

This Accessibility Statement describes our commitment to accessibility, the accessibility features of our Site, and the steps we are taking to ensure compliance with applicable accessibility standards and regulations.

Our Commitment to Accessibility

Accessibility as a Priority

We are committed to making our Site accessible to all users, including those with visual, auditory, motor, and cognitive disabilities. Accessibility is an ongoing process, and we are dedicated to continuously improving our website to meet or exceed accessibility standards and best practices.

Inclusive Design Principles

We design and develop our Site with accessibility in mind from the beginning. We follow inclusive design principles to ensure that our content and functionality are usable by everyone, regardless of their abilities or the assistive technologies they use.

Accessibility Team

We have designated team members responsible for monitoring and improving the accessibility of our Site. These team members work to identify accessibility issues, implement improvements, and respond to accessibility feedback from users.

Standards and Compliance

WCAG 2.1 Level AA Compliance

We strive to conform to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA published by the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C). WCAG 2.1 provides recommendations for making web content more accessible to people with disabilities. Level AA represents a mid-range level of accessibility compliance and is widely recognized as a standard for web accessibility.

Legal Compliance

Our Site is designed to comply with applicable accessibility laws and regulations, including:

  • Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA): Title II and Title III of the ADA require that websites operated by public entities and businesses be accessible to people with disabilities
  • Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act: Requires federal agencies and contractors to provide accessible electronic and information technology
  • State and Local Accessibility Laws: We comply with Virginia and other applicable state and local accessibility requirements

Accessibility Features

Visual Accessibility

Our Site includes the following visual accessibility features:

  • Color Contrast: Text and interactive elements have sufficient color contrast (at least 4.5:1 for normal text and 3:1 for large text) to be readable by people with low vision
  • Resizable Text: Users can resize text using browser controls without loss of functionality or content
  • Readable Fonts: We use clear, sans-serif fonts that are easy to read
  • Adequate Spacing: Content is properly spaced to improve readability
  • No Flashing Content: We avoid flashing or blinking content that could trigger seizures

Screen Reader Compatibility

Our Site is designed to work with screen readers and other assistive technologies:

  • Semantic HTML: We use proper semantic HTML structure (headings, lists, landmarks) to provide meaningful structure for screen readers
  • Alt Text for Images: All images include descriptive alt text that conveys the purpose and content of the image
  • Form Labels: All form fields have associated labels that are properly marked up for screen readers
  • ARIA Attributes: We use ARIA (Accessible Rich Internet Applications) attributes where appropriate to enhance accessibility of dynamic content
  • Skip Links: Skip navigation links allow users to bypass repetitive content and jump directly to main content

Keyboard Navigation

Our Site is fully navigable using keyboard controls:

  • Tab Navigation: All interactive elements (links, buttons, form fields) are accessible via the Tab key in a logical order
  • Focus Indicators: Keyboard focus is clearly visible with visible focus indicators
  • No Keyboard Traps: Users can navigate away from any element using the keyboard without getting trapped
  • Keyboard Shortcuts: Common keyboard shortcuts are supported (e.g., Enter to activate buttons, Space for checkboxes)

Responsive Design

Our Site is responsive and works well on all device sizes and screen resolutions:

  • Content reflows properly on smaller screens without horizontal scrolling
  • Touch targets are appropriately sized for mobile device use
  • The Site is usable in both portrait and landscape orientations
  • Zoom functionality works properly without breaking layout

Multimedia Accessibility

Multimedia content on our Site includes accessibility features:

  • Video Captions: Videos include captions for people who are deaf or hard of hearing
  • Audio Descriptions: Important visual information in videos is described for people who are blind or have low vision
  • Transcripts: Transcripts are provided for audio and video content

Content Accessibility

Our content is written and structured for accessibility:

  • Clear Language: We use clear, simple language and avoid jargon when possible
  • Proper Heading Structure: Content is organized with proper heading hierarchy (H1, H2, H3, etc.)
  • Lists: Information is organized into lists when appropriate for clarity
  • Link Text: Links have descriptive text that explains their purpose (not "click here")
  • Page Titles: Each page has a descriptive title that identifies its purpose

Form Accessibility

Forms on our Site are designed for accessibility:

  • All form fields have associated labels
  • Required fields are clearly marked
  • Error messages are clear and helpful
  • Form instructions are provided where needed
  • Forms can be navigated and submitted using keyboard alone

Known Limitations and Exceptions

Third-Party Content

While we strive to make all content accessible, some third-party content embedded on our Site (such as external videos, maps, or social media widgets) may have limited accessibility features. We are working with third-party providers to improve the accessibility of their content and will update our Site as better alternatives become available.

Legacy Content

Some older content on our Site may not fully meet current accessibility standards. We are gradually updating legacy content to improve accessibility. If you encounter inaccessible legacy content, please contact us so we can prioritize its remediation.

PDF Documents

PDF documents on our Site may have varying levels of accessibility. We are working to ensure that all PDFs are properly tagged and accessible. If you have difficulty accessing a PDF, please contact us and we can provide the information in an alternative format.

Browser and Technology Limitations

Accessibility depends on both our Site and the technologies you use to access it. We support current versions of major browsers and assistive technologies. If you experience accessibility issues, please verify that you are using a current browser version and let us know about the problem.

Accessibility Testing and Monitoring

Regular Testing

We conduct regular accessibility testing of our Site using:

  • Automated accessibility testing tools to identify potential issues
  • Manual testing with assistive technologies (screen readers, keyboard navigation)
  • User testing with people with disabilities
  • Third-party accessibility audits and reviews

Continuous Improvement

We are committed to continuously improving the accessibility of our Site. We monitor accessibility standards and best practices and update our Site accordingly. We also incorporate accessibility feedback from users into our improvement process.

Accessibility Roadmap

We maintain an accessibility roadmap that outlines our plans for improving Site accessibility. This roadmap includes timelines for addressing known accessibility issues and implementing new accessibility features.

Assistive Technologies and Tools

Supported Assistive Technologies

Our Site is designed to work with common assistive technologies, including:

  • Screen Readers: NVDA, JAWS, VoiceOver, TalkBack
  • Magnification Software: ZoomText, built-in browser zoom
  • Speech Recognition: Dragon NaturallySpeaking, built-in OS speech recognition
  • Keyboard Navigation: All major operating systems

Browser Extensions

Users may also use browser extensions and tools to enhance accessibility, such as:

  • Text-to-speech extensions
  • Color contrast adjustment tools
  • Reading mode extensions
  • Password managers and autofill tools

Reporting Accessibility Issues

How to Report Issues

If you encounter any accessibility barriers or have difficulty accessing content on our Site, please report it to us. We take accessibility feedback seriously and will work to resolve issues promptly.

You can report accessibility issues by:

  • Emailing us at info@rickyhorsemancompanies.com with "Accessibility Issue" in the subject line
  • Calling us at (703) 737-0713 and asking to speak with our accessibility team
  • Filling out our contact form and describing the accessibility issue

Information to Include

When reporting an accessibility issue, please include:

  • The specific page or feature that is inaccessible
  • A description of the accessibility barrier you encountered
  • The assistive technology or browser you are using
  • Your contact information so we can follow up with you

Response Timeline

We aim to acknowledge accessibility reports within 2 business days and provide an initial assessment within 5 business days. We will work with you to resolve the issue and provide alternative solutions if needed.

Alternative Formats

If you cannot access content on our Site due to an accessibility issue, we can provide the information in an alternative format, such as:

  • Large print documents
  • Braille documents
  • Audio recordings
  • Plain text versions
  • Other formats as needed
