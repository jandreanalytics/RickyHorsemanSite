Specialty Concrete & Sport Courts

Concrete courts work for basketball, pickleball, tennis, volleyball, and recreational play. We design markings and dimensions based on the sport(s) you want.

Space requirements vary by sport. A full basketball court is roughly 47x50 feet; pickleball is 20x44 feet; tennis is 36x78 feet. We work with whatever space you have and discuss realistic options.

Cost depends on size, site conditions, and surface finish. A basic 30x40 court might run $4,000–$8,000+; larger courts or those with specialty coatings will cost more. We provide a detailed quote after discussing your specific needs.

A sealed court uses protective sealer on the concrete base—simpler, more affordable, good for general play. A coated court includes a specialized sport surface (acrylic or similar)—improves traction and durability for competitive play. We discuss which matches your needs and budget.

A well-built concrete court lasts 15–25+ years with proper maintenance. Sealed surfaces may need resealing every few years. Specialty coatings may need occasional recoating depending on use and weather.
