How We Build Your Court

A great sport court starts with careful planning and expert execution. We follow the same straightforward approach for every project, with special attention to the precision required for courts.

1

Court Consultation & Design

We meet you on-site to understand what you want. We discuss sport type, court dimensions, drainage strategy, and finish preferences. We ask questions about how you'll use the space and what surface will work best.

2

Detailed Plan & Quote

We provide specifications including exact dimensions, base requirements, drainage details, and finish options. We give you a clear quote with no surprises and a realistic timeline so you know what to expect.

3

Preparation & Installation

Proper base preparation is critical for court longevity. We handle grading, base materials, and drainage carefully. During the pour, we ensure the concrete is properly mixed, placed, and finished to tournament-level flatness and slope specifications.

4

Finishing & Sealing

Once cured, we apply protective sealers, verify all dimensions and surface standards, and discuss maintenance. Your court is ready to play on immediately.

Questions About Sport Courts

Concrete courts work for basketball, pickleball, tennis, volleyball, and recreational multi-sport play. We design markings and dimensions based on the sport(s) you want to play. We can also build multi-purpose courts that accommodate several activities.

Space requirements vary by sport. A full basketball court is roughly 47x50 feet; pickleball is 20x44 feet; tennis is 36x78 feet. We work with whatever space you have and discuss whether full-court, half-court, or modified dimensions make sense for your property.

Cost depends on size, base conditions, location, and surface finish. A basic 30x40 court might run $4,000–$8,000+; larger courts or those with specialty coatings will cost more. We provide a detailed quote after discussing your specific needs and site conditions.

A sealed court uses a protective sealer on the concrete base—simpler and more affordable, good for general play. A coated court includes a specialized sport surface (acrylic or similar)—improves traction, reduces glare, and adds durability for competitive play. We discuss which option matches your needs and budget.

A well-built concrete court lasts 15–25+ years with proper maintenance. Sealed surfaces may need resealing every few years. Specialty coatings may need occasional recoating depending on use and weather. We provide maintenance guidance after your court is complete.
